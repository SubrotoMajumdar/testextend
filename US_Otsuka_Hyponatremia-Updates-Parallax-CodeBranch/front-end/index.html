About hyponatremia

Hyponatremia is a common electrolyte disorder4

Hyponatremia is a common electrolyte disorder that occurs in up to 30% of all hospitalized patients4.The disorder is important to recognize because it can have serious clinical consequences.5

Hyponatremia can be defined by:

  • Serum [Na+] concentration <135 mEq/L6
  • Disorder of water balance
    • Body water is in excess relative to total body sodium2

Types of Hyponatremia

Hyponatremia can be classified by several clinical parameters, and treatment strategies are often based on a combination these parameters.2,7

Incidence of hyponatremia is high in hospitalized patients1

Using the definition of serum [Na+] < 135 mEq/L, reported incidences of hyponatremia in both acutely and chronically hospitalized patients have been as high as 15 to 30%.1

Etiology and the role of vasopressin in hypotonic hyponatremia

Hypotonic hyponatremia has multiple etiologies2,8,9

Etiologies can be categorized under the 3 main volume statuses: hypovolemic hyponatremia, euvolemic hyponatremia, and hypervolemic hyponatermia. Laboratory data and a physical exam will help assess patients volume status. The volume status will help determine the etiology of the hyponatremia2,8,9.

Hypotonic hyponatremia typically results from a disruption in the free water clearance from the kidneys, which is usually due to vasopressin release or renal failure.10

Hyponatremia Symptoms, Complications, and Risks

Hyponatremia can result in poor patient outcomes due to the following complications and risks:

  • Adverse effects on the brain1,5,7,11,12
  • Increased mortality risk13
  • Increased falls and fractures14-19

Hyponatremia signs and symptoms can range from mild or nonspecific to severe

Non-specific or mild symptoms of hyponatremia can progress quickly to severe symptoms. The more severe symptoms are often neurologic complications due to brain edema and increased intracranial pressure from severe and rapidly evolving hyponatremia1,5,7,11

Hyponatremia treatment

There are several treatment options available for the treatment of hyponatremia

Below is a list of many of the treatment options that are currently used.

  • Fluid Restriction2,24
  • Hypertonic Saline1,24
  • Isotonic Saline1
  • Loop Diuretics1,2,24
  • Demeclocycline1,2,24
  • Urea1
  • Lithium2,24
  • Vasopressin Receptor Antagonists (Vaptans)1,24

Hyponatremia treatment approach depends on criteria such as hyponatremia rate of onset,symptom status, and status 1,11
